Who it's for

Designed for the most demanding signal pipelines.

Raw signal data is generated faster than it can be stored or transmitted. NOEMA helps reduce the cost of keeping high-value I/Q data available for replay, analysis, and future processing.

Autonomous Vehicles
AV test fleets generate terabytes of raw radar data daily.

Storing everything is expensive; discarding captures can mean losing edge cases. NOEMA compresses raw sensor streams while preserving high-fidelity reconstruction for later replay and analysis.

Satellite and SAR
Downlink windows are measured in minutes. Every reduction in data volume matters.

NOEMA compresses Level-0 SAR and signal data at the source, on the ground, or between relay and archive layers. More data can move through the same infrastructure.

Defense and SIGINT
At the edge, storage is constrained and connectivity is intermittent.

NOEMA compresses at the point of collection. Air-gappable, ARM-compatible, and built for replay workflows where reconstruction fidelity matters.

Technical advantages

What the architecture makes possible.

Region-based access

Indexed sidecar metadata supports decoding a specific scene or region without reconstructing the full file first. Reconstructed signal data can feed existing replay, analysis, and processing workflows directly.

Shared codebook

A single codebook can serve a batch of files instead of one per file, reducing overhead and supporting consistent reconstruction across an archive.

Auditability

Sidecar metadata records what was compressed, how it was reconstructed, what fidelity was measured, and what region index data is available. SNR, RMSE, and correlation are computed and reported for every file.

Less data movement

Researchers can avoid pulling an entire raw capture just to inspect one region or run one test.
