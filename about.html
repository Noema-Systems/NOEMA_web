About

NOEMA addresses a core constraint in modern sensing systems: raw signal data grows faster than it can be stored, transferred, or processed at scale.

In radar, RF, and SAR pipelines, this creates escalating storage costs, bandwidth bottlenecks, and reduced ability to retain full fidelity historical data for later analysis.

NOEMA reduces this burden by compressing raw signal data into smaller, indexed working archives that remain reconstructable and usable in downstream workflows.

The system operates directly on signal structure rather than treating data as generic byte streams, allowing compression to adapt to the statistical properties of each dataset.

Design Principles
Compression First

Primary value is reduction in raw signal storage and transfer cost at scale.

Fidelity-Audited

Reconstruction quality is measured using signal-level metrics and verification checks.

Indexed Access

Archives remain structured so specific regions can be accessed without full decoding.

Operational Fit

Designed to integrate into existing pipelines without requiring workflow redesign.

Context

Modern sensing systems generate continuous streams of high-volume signal data that are expensive to store and difficult to retain in full resolution.

NOEMA is built for environments where storage efficiency, reproducibility, and long-term access to raw signals directly affect operational capability.
